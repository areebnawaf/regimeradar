Why extend to crypto?

Hirsa et al. (2024) demonstrated R2-RD on macroeconomic indicators and futures markets. This tab applies the same rolling ensemble methodology to Bitcoin — a 24/7, higher-volatility asset where regime instability is more pronounced. Because crypto lacks a VIX equivalent, realized volatility serves as the fear proxy. This extension tests whether R2-RD generalizes beyond its original domain.

What is market regime detection?

Financial markets cycle through distinct behavioral states — bull expansions, stress crises, transitions, and recoveries. Identifying the current regime in real time allows investors to adjust risk exposure before conditions deteriorate. The core challenge: markets are nonstationary, meaning the statistical fingerprint of "stress" in 2008 differs from 2020 or 2022.

The R2-RD innovation (Hirsa, Xu & Malhotra 2024)

Standard models like Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) or Hidden Markov Models trained once become stale as markets evolve — they suffer from temporal instability. R2-RD solves this with three mechanisms: (1) adaptive rolling retraining as new data streams in, (2) temporal ensemble voting that smooths label instability across a lookback window, and (3) data-driven model selection that picks the best-fitting latent variable model from a candidate pool rather than assuming one fixed structure.

How RegimeRadar implements it

This tool uses rolling z-scores of the VIX and S&P 500 20-day momentum as the two-dimensional feature space. A rolling classifier with ensemble smoothing (window = 5) assigns regime labels. The confidence score (0–100) measures label stability across a 10-day trailing ensemble — low confidence signals regime transitions. The crypto tab substitutes realized volatility for VIX, testing generalizability.

Measurable output: regime confidence score

Rather than a binary regime label, RegimeRadar outputs a continuous confidence score. A score above 75 indicates a stable, well-defined regime. Below 40 signals a transition — historically the most important risk management signal, as Hirsa et al. show mutual fund return dispersion widens sharply during these periods. This turns an academic framework into a practical, interpretable daily tool.
