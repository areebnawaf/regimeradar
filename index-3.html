Extension rationale

Hirsa et al. (2024) demonstrated R2-RD on macroeconomic indicators and traditional futures markets. This tab extends the methodology to Bitcoin — a 24/7, higher-volatility asset where regime transitions are more abrupt. Because crypto lacks a VIX equivalent, 30-day realized volatility serves as the fear proxy, with VIXY used for cross-market calibration. This tests whether R2-RD's rolling retraining generalizes to assets with fundamentally different microstructure.

R2-RD Methodology
Robust Rolling Regime Detection · Data-driven implementation
STEP 01 · FEATURE ENGINEERING
Two-dimensional state space
VIX rolling z-score captures fear levels relative to recent history. 20-day SPY momentum captures trend direction. Together they form the feature space that R2-RD uses to separate latent market states. Realized volatility and drawdown serve as auxiliary features.
STEP 02 · ROLLING GMM
Adaptive retraining on each window
A Gaussian Mixture Model (n=4 components) is retrained on a 120-day rolling window of daily feature data. This ensures the statistical fingerprint of each regime updates as market structure evolves — the core innovation over static, once-trained models.
STEP 03 · ECONOMIC LABELING
Cluster → regime mapping
Raw GMM cluster IDs are mapped to economic regimes (Bull, Stress, Transition, Recovery) by inspecting cluster means. High VIX z-score + negative momentum → Stress. Positive momentum + low VIX → Bull. This avoids the label-switching problem inherent in unsupervised models.
STEP 04 · TEMPORAL ENSEMBLE
Smoothing label chatter
A 5-day majority-vote window smooths the raw daily labels, suppressing short-lived misclassifications. The confidence score (0–100) measures the 10-day trailing label stability — below 50 signals a likely regime transition and is the most actionable output of the model.

Differences from the original paper

The Hirsa et al. (2024) paper applies R2-RD primarily to macroeconomic factor data (yield curve, credit spreads, equity risk premium). This implementation uses daily price and ETF data (SPY, VIXY) as a practical approximation accessible without Bloomberg terminals. The rolling GMM mechanism, temporal ensemble smoothing, and 4-regime taxonomy are faithful to the paper. The BTC extension is original work building on the R2-RD framework.

Citation
